# Persistence, Robustness, and Decay of the Customer-Supplier Momentum Anomaly

## Findings

- **Original result.** Cohen & Frazzini (2008), *Economic Links and Predictable Returns*, *Journal of Finance* 63(4), 1977-2011 (SSRN: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2758776; Wiley: https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1540-6261.2008.01379.x). Long-short strategy buying suppliers of "winner" customers and shorting suppliers of "loser" customers earns >150 bps/month; alpha is largely unaffected by controls for the 3-factor model, liquidity, own-firm momentum, industry momentum, and lead-lag effects (per AQR's own summary: https://www.aqr.com/Insights/Research/Journal-Article/Economic-Links-and-Predictable-Returns). The paper won the JF's Smith Breeden Prize.

- **Direct evidence of decay/post-discovery decline.** Pinchuk, "Customer Momentum" (arXiv:2301.11394, Jan 2023; https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.11394): splits the sample into pre- and post-discovery (i.e., pre/post Cohen-Frazzini publication) periods. Pre-discovery, value-weighted long-short decile alpha is roughly 106-122 bps/month (t > 2.8-4). Post-discovery, the strategy "has a smaller magnitude and loses statistical significance" — consistent with arbitrage activity eroding the anomaly after publication. The same source (via search summary, not directly verified in the abstract text) cites a comparison of ~0.79%/month vs. the original ~1.58%/month at a 1-month horizon in later replications — could not independently confirm this specific number in the primary text, so treat it as approximate. Pinchuk also finds customer momentum is "partially driven by the lead-lag relationship between small and large stocks," implying size/liquidity concentration, and that it is largely independent of price and earnings momentum.

- **General publication-decay backdrop.** McLean & Pontiff, "Does Academic Research Destroy Stock Return Predictability?", *Journal of Finance* 71(1), 2016 (SSRN: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=2156623). Across 97 anomalies, returns are ~26% lower out-of-sample and ~58% lower post-publication, with effects concentrated in illiquid/high-idiosyncratic-risk stocks — exactly the segment where customer-supplier links are found. Did **not** find explicit confirmation that Cohen-Frazzini's specific factor was one of the 97 anomalies; verify against their anomaly list before citing it as direct evidence for this specific effect.

- **Mechanism/attention refinement.** Madsen, "Anticipated Earnings Announcements and the Customer–Supplier Anomaly," *Journal of Accounting Research* 55(3), 2017, 709-741 (DOI 10.1111/1475-679X.12158; https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/1475-679X.12158). Shows customer returns predict supplier returns *before* but not *after* the supplier's own earnings announcement — attention to customer news rises around scheduled announcements, resolving limited attention and shrinking the exploitable window. Robustness/mechanism paper, not a decay study, but implies the anomaly's window is time-varying and attention-dependent.

- **Closest cousins — magnitude and persistence:**
  - Menzly & Ozbas, "Market Segmentation and Cross-Predictability of Returns," *Journal of Finance* 65(4), 2010, 1555-1580 (https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/abs/10.1111/j.1540-6261.2010.01578.x). Cross-industry (supplier/customer industry) momentum; magnitude declines with analyst coverage and institutional ownership — an explicit attention/limits-to-arbitrage gradient.
  - Hou, "Industry Information Diffusion and the Lead-Lag Effect in Stock Returns," *Review of Financial Studies* 20(4), 2007, 1113-1138 (https://academic.oup.com/rfs/article-abstract/20/4/1113/1615954). Lead-lag is predominantly intra-industry, big-lead-small, and stronger in small/neglected/less-competitive industries — a direct small-cap/illiquidity concentration finding.
  - Cohen & Lou, "Complicated Firms," *Journal of Financial Economics* 104(2), 2012 (https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0304405X11001899). "Pseudo-conglomerate" portfolio return predicts conglomerate return next month (a 1-SD move -> 53 bps), robust to industry-momentum controls — same underlying attention-based mechanism family.
  - Shahrur, Becker & Rosenfeld, "Return Predictability Along the Supply Chain: The International Evidence," *Financial Analysts Journal* 66(3), 2010, 60-77 (https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.2469/faj.v66.n3.8). Confirms customer->supplier lead-lag across 22 developed markets — external validity outside the US sample.
  - Ali & Hirshleifer, "Shared Analyst Coverage: Unifying Momentum Spillover Effects," *Journal of Financial Economics* 136(3), 2020, 649-675 (NBER w25201: https://www.nber.org/papers/w25201). Important critique: a shared-analyst-coverage momentum factor (1.68%/month, t=9.67) **subsumes** customer/supplier-industry momentum and several other spillover anomalies — in spanning regressions their alphas become insignificant or negative once shared-analyst-coverage momentum is controlled for. Suggests the customer-supplier effect may be a symptom of a more general, possibly more persistent, analyst-attention-linkage phenomenon rather than a standalone factor.

## Uncertainties / gaps
- Pinchuk (2023) is a working paper (not confirmed peer-reviewed as of search) — treat its exact bps figures (esp. "0.79% vs 1.58%") as approximate pending verification against the full PDF/tables.
- Could not confirm whether Cohen-Frazzini's factor is explicitly among McLean & Pontiff's 97 anomalies or Hou-Xue-Zhang's "Replicating Anomalies" set. Do not cite either as direct post-publication performance for this specific factor without checking their appendices.
- No direct, recent (2020s) single paper measuring a live/current-decade alpha for the exact Cohen-Frazzini construction was found — closest is Pinchuk's pre/post split, not a rolling recent-years-only estimate.
- Factor-decay/capacity area moves fast and is thinly published in top journals (more preprints than peer-reviewed replications) — treat any recent-decade numbers as provisional, recheck against a WRDS/Compustat replication before use.
